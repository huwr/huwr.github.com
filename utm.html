What on Earth is UTM?

Great question!

UTM stands for 'Universal Transverse Mercator'. It is a mapping system like the traditional system of Latitude and Longitude. Instead of the co-ordinates being in degrees, though, UTM works in metres. These are Northing and Easting. Rather than using only 1 meridian, UTM divides the globe into 60 Zones, each with 6Â° of longitude.

We specify a location in UTM by giving all three factors. Here's an example:

  Easting: 228854 m
  Northing: 5828259 m
  Zone: 55

Although, usually, specifying all the digits is too specific. There's also a 6-digit shorthand! The shorthand specifies the 3rd, 4th and 5th digits for Easting and Northing, and omits the Zone. Easting goes first. The example for above is: 

288828
