Huw’s Cryptanalysis Challenge Answer 2

By Huw

This is a polyalphabetic cipher. It is very similar to the solution for the first cipher, but it uses multiple alphabets.

The first letter is shifted by some amount, the second by a different amount, and so on, in sets of 5. The amount each letter is shifted is determined by a key. The solution to the first cipher gave away the key word: WALES.

This was the most challenging of the three ciphers.

Plain

If you can keep your head when all about you
Are losing theirs and blaming it on you,
If you can trust yourself when all men doubt you,
But make allowance for their doubting too;
If you can wait and not be tired by waiting,
Or being lied about, don’t deal in lies,
Or being hated, don’t give way to hating,
And yet don’t look too good, nor talk too wise:

This is the first stanza of If—, a poem by Rudyard Kipling in 1895.
