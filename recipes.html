Quail Portuguese

Simple enough meal. Serve on a bed of flavoured rice. Allow 2 quails per person for hearty appetites.

Ingredients

  • 6–8 quails
  • Rind of 1 orange, peeled thinly
  • Salt and pepper
  • 30 g butter
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (or orange juice)
  • Flavoured rice (see: Rice Portuguese)

Procedure

Dry the quails, put a piece of orange rind in each, and season with salt and pepper. Tie each one neatly with string so that they will keep their shape during cooking. Place them in an overpoof casserole with the butter and wine (or orange juice), cover and cook in a very hot oven (240Â° C) for 20 minutes.

Turn the Rice Portuguese onto a serving dish, untie the quails and set them on top of the rice. Swirl the juices in the casserole to mix will, and serve them over the rice. Serves 4–6.

Rice Portuguese

Makes an excellent accompaniment to roast chicken or quail.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, peeled and chopped
  • 90 g butter
  • 2 cups long-grained rice
  • 3 cups of chicken stock, hot
  • 2 tomatoes, peeled, seeded and diced
  • 1 capsicum, seeded and diced

Procedure

Fry the onion gently in half the butter until a golden colour. Add the rice and cook, stirring, over a medium heat until the rice is coated with the butter. Add the hot stock, tomatoes and capsicum. Bring to the boil, reduce heat, then cover with a lid and cook about 18 minutes. As soon as it is cooked, carfully fork in the remaining butter. Serves 4–6.

Chocolate Sauce Pudding

Don't use a spring-form pan. That will make a mess and everyone will make fun of you again…

(This is a very old recipe...)

Ingredients

  • 60 g butter
  • 1/2 cup castor sugar
  • dash vanilla essence
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon cocoa
  • 1 cup self-raising flour
  • 1 cup milk

Procedure

Topping

Combine in a saucepan and heat until boiling the following: 1/2 cup sugar, 1 tablespoon cocoa, 1 1/2 cup boiling water

Main

Cream butter, sugar and essence. Add egg, beat until fluffy. Fold in half the sifted cocoa and self-raising flour. Repeat with remaining flour, cocoa and milk mix. Pour mixture into a 1.5 litre capacity oven proof baking dish (not a spring-form pan). Carefully pour the topping over the cake batter. Bake in a hot oven for 30-40 minutes (the cake batter will rise to the surface and the topping will form a sauce underneath). Serve hot

Tom Yum Gung

Yummy Thai hot and sour soup. 4 or 6 people.

Ingredients

  • 450 g large prawns with shells
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1.2 L cold water
  • 3 tamarind slices
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2-4 fresh or dried chillies
  • 2.5 cm ginger root
  • 2.5 cm galingale
  • 1 stalk fresh lemon grass cut into three
  • 1 tbs peanut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 600 mL strong, clear chook stock (optional)
  • 2 carrots
  • 1 small white radish
  • 120 g mustard greens or watercres
  • 60 g oyster mushrooms, or 30 g woodear fungus
  • 1 tbs fish sauce

Procedure

Peel and devein the prawns. Wash the prawns, and the shells, very thoroughly. Put the prawns on a plate, sprinkle with half the salt and keep in the fridge until needed. Put the shells aside.

Put the water into a large saucepan with the tamarind slices, chillies, kaffir lime leaves, ginger, galingale, lemon grass and the rest of the salt. Bring this to the boil and simmer for 20 to 30 minutes. By this time the stock will be very fragrant from the lemon grass and other aromatic ingredients.

In a small saucepan, heat the oil and fry the chopped onion until soft, then add the prawn shells and stir for about 2 minutes. Put the onion and prawn shells, without any oil, into the stock pot. Continue to simmer for 15 minutes. Then strain the stock through a fine sieve, or a sieve lined with muslin, into another large saucepan. Discard the solids. Add the chicken stock, if used, or water or vegetable stock (this additional liquid is needed if the soup is to serve 6 people). Keep aside to reheat later.

While the hot and sour stock is brewing, prepare the vegetables. Peel the carrots and white radish and cut them into thin rounds. If the white radish is very large, cut the rounds in half. Pick over and wash the mustard greens or watercress. Shred the oyster mushrooms. If using dried mushrooms, soak these in boiling water for 30 minutes, then remove the stalks; put the stalks and the water into the stock pot. Slice the mushrooms, which are now quite soft.

Put the clear and strained stock to boil gently. Add the carrots and white radish and mushrooms. Simmer for 10 minutes. Increase the heat and bring the stock to a rolling boil, then add the prawns. Let the mixture boil for 3 minutes, and add the mustard greens or watercress and fish sauce. Cook for the soup for just one more minute, then serve immediately.
